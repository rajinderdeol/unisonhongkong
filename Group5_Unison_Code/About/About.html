ABOUT UNISON AND OUR VISION

What is Unison?

Hong Kong Unison is a non-governmental organization founded in March 2001 and recognized as a public charitable institution (IR File No.91/7763)in 2005. We receive no government funding. In the past years, our services have expanded from handigh individual cases to advocating policy reforms for ethic minority residents.

How We Began?

"To act justly, and love mercy", this is the value that propelled Unison's founding director, Fermi Wong, to incessantly advocate for ethic minority residents in Hong Kong. She was awarded the 2012 Hong Kong Humanity Award in recognition of her long term contribution to humanity through serving the vulnerable. Read about the story of Unison and the values of social work in the book chapter by Fermi Wong.

Who are Our Service Targets?

Hong Kong Unison focuses its work on serving ethnic minority Hong Kong residents and their families.

Apart from permanent residents, ethnic minorities in Hong Kong also include foreign domestic workers, refugees/ asymium seekers/ torture claimants and expatriate workers. There are other organizations with services catered to the latter three groups. To contact other organizations with services catered to the latter three groups. Our Mission

In regards to secular and harmious society, we are concerned with elimination of racial discrimination, having equal education opportunities and employment & training opportunities for our ethnic minoirty community. We want to also the enhancement of cultural & racial sensitivity. Our fight is for equal access to public services for everyone, and also empowerment & social participation of ethnic minority residence in Hong Kon. We have four main missions!

Our Work Scope & Strategies

After years of evaluation, Hong Kong Unison now puts most of our effort in Policy Advocacy work. That includes advocating for better Chinese language education policies for ethnic minorities, expanding ethnic minority students' post-secondary options in education, and fighting for equal access to public services.

Hong Kong Unison also operates some direct services, such as:
  1. Promoting racial harmony and cultural sensitivity in schools;
  2. Giving talks on cultural sensitivity to interested organizations;
  3. Organizing career guidance programs for ethnic minoriry students;
  4. Scholarships for ethnic minority students who wish to further their education.
